Design rules (lodged in odc-ui skill): binding actions never execute from feed context — ballot and action cards are doors only. Pinned ballot bar sits outside feed ranking (deterministic surfacing). Sentiment-lane sort orders are user-selectable and transparent; no engagement-optimized default. Lane identity never rests on color alone — chips carry text and icons for colorblind accessibility.

Variant C notes: tinted backgrounds give the strongest lane separation and make the feed feel segmented rather than uniform — good for scanability, but it is the loudest option: a long feed of colored blocks can read as busy, and the ballot loses its "only colored thing on screen" status entirely, so its solid-fill CTA button now carries the emphasis instead. Text on tints uses dark same-family stops for contrast.

Variant trade-off (A vs B): A preserves hierarchy — the ballot's accent border is the only color, so importance and color are one signal. B gives instant lane recognition but ballots compete with purple/teal for attention. Suggested middle path: colored chips for lane identity, neutral card borders except the ballot's single emphasized border. Decide with real content density on screen.
